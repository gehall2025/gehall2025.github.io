The Origin of Matcha

Matcha is a finely ground green tea powder that has been part of Japanese culture for centuries.

About This Object

Matcha has its roots in the 12th century when the Buddhist monk Eisai brought powedered tea from China to Japan. Monks used the drink to stay awake and focused during long meditation sessions.

Over time, matcha became an important part of Japanese culture and was used in the Japanese tea ceremony, a ritual that emphaszied mindfulness, rescpect, and harmony. What began as a spiritual drink in temples eventually grew into a symbol of tradition and cultural identity.
