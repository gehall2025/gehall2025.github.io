About This Object

During the 20th and 21st centuries, matcha began appearing internationally as Japanese culture spread worldwide. Tea producers exported matcha to new audiences, introducing its flavor and cultural significance beyond tea rooms.

Cafes, restaurants, and global food brands incorporated matcha into desserts, drinks, and pastries. Companies like Starbucks helped popularize matcha beverages, transforming this ceremonial tea into a globally recognized ingredient while mainaining its connection to Japanese tradition.
