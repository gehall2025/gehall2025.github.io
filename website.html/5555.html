History of Tea

Tea has a long and rich history originating in East Asia. It has been cultivated, consumed, and celebrated for thousands of years, evolving from medicinal use to a deeply cultural and social beverage.

Effects of Tea / Benefits of Tea

Tea is widely appreciated for its calming effects and natural compounds. It is often associated with relaxation, focus, and antioxidants that support overall well-being.

Storage of Tea

Proper storage is essential to preserve tea’s flavor and quality. Tea should be kept in a cool, dry place away from light, moisture, and strong odors.

Kinds of Tea

Tea comes in many varieties, including green tea, black tea, white tea, oolong, and matcha. Each type is defined by its processing method and flavor profile.

How Sencha and Gyokuro are made

Sencha and Gyokuro are premium Japanese green teas. Sencha is grown in full sunlight and steamed after harvest, while Gyokuro is shade-grown, creating a sweeter and more delicate flavor.

How Matcha is processed

Matcha is made from shade-grown tea leaves that are carefully steamed, dried, and stone-ground into a fine powder. This process preserves color, nutrients, and flavor.

Enjoy Tea

Preparation of Matcha

Matcha is prepared by whisking fine powder with hot water using a bamboo whisk until it becomes frothy and smooth.

Preparation of Gyokuro

Gyokuro is brewed at low temperatures to highlight its natural sweetness and umami flavor.

Preparation of Sencha

Sencha is brewed with moderately hot water and steeped briefly to preserve its fresh, grassy taste.

Water for Tea

The quality and temperature of water greatly influence tea flavor. Soft water is often preferred for Japanese teas.

Matcha a la carte

Matcha can be enjoyed traditionally or incorporated into modern recipes such as lattes, desserts, and smoothies.
