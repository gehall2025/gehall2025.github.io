About This Object

Preparing matcha is both a craft and a ritual. Traditional tools include the bamboo whisk (Chasen), tea bowl (Chawan), and tea scoop (Chashaku). The powdered tea is whisked with hot water until it forms a smooth, frothy drink.

This process is central to Chanoyu, the Japanese tea ceremony, a carefully choreographed ritual that emphasizes mindfulness, respect, and hospitality. Each step—from scooping the tea to whisking the foam—reflects centuries of tradition and attention to detail.
